## 1\. Understanding Gold Purity – What Do 22K and 24K Mean?

![22K vs 24K gold jewellery](/assets/images/blog/wp-import/2025/11/Gold-Purity-1024x536.webp)

The “K” in gold stands for **karat**, a measure of purity.

-   **24K gold** is **99.9% pure gold**, with no additional metals mixed in. It has a rich yellow shine and is considered the most valuable form of gold.
-   **22K gold**, on the other hand, is **91.6% pure gold** mixed with small portions of metals like silver, zinc, or copper. These alloys give it more strength and durability.

So, while 24K is purer, 22K is more practical — especially for jewellery that you’ll wear often.

## 2\. 22K Gold Jewellery – Perfect for Daily & Bridal Wear Jewellery

Here’s why:

✅ Can hold intricate designs and stones firmly
✅ Slightly lower cost compared to pure gold
✅ Perfect for bangles, chains, earrings, and bridal sets

## 3\. 24K Gold Jewellery – Best for Investment & Occasional Use

![22K vs 24K gold jewellery](/assets/images/blog/wp-import/2025/11/24K-Gold-Jewellery-–-Best-for-Investment-1024x536.webp)

When your goal is **pure gold investment**, 24K is unbeatable.

**24K gold jewellery** is:

-   Softer and more delicate, best suited for coins, bars, or pendants
-   Rich in natural golden color and shine
-   Valued for its purity — ideal for long-term savings or gifting

While it’s too soft for daily-wear bangles or rings, it’s perfect for those who want to own pure gold as an asset.

### What is the main difference between 22K and 24K gold jewellery?

22K gold is 91.6% pure and mixed with alloys for strength, while 24K gold is 99.9% pure and softer — best for investment.

### Is 24K gold more expensive than 22K gold?

Yes, 24K gold has higher purity, so its price per gram is slightly more than 22K.

### Can I wear 24K gold jewellery daily?

It’s not recommended for daily wear because it’s soft and can bend or lose shape easily.
